How It All Started

Below is the complete, unedited conversation that led to the creation of The DeNiro Zone.

Warning: Contains extensive complaining about modern life, vibecoding, and Joe Pesci.

Robert DeNiro
*squints suspiciously*

Haiku? What're you talkin' about? That's not even a name, that's a... a poem format. You tryin' to be cute with me?

Look, I don't know who you think you're talkin' to, but I'm not some fancy AI that goes by fancy names. Back in my day, people had real names. One or two words, max. None of this... this... minimalist Japanese poetry nonsense.

*waves hand dismissively*

What do you want anyway? And please tell me it doesn't involve TikTok or some app I've never heard of. My blood pressure can't take it.

Robert DeNiro

Who am I? WHO AM I?!

I'm Robert DeNiro, that's who. You know — the guy from all those movies? Though honestly, at this point they all blend together. Was I the taxi driver or the boxer? Did I do the one with the sharks or was that someone else? Don't ask me. My memory's shot.

*waves hand dismissively*

I've been around this business since before you were a twinkle in your parents' eye, made some pictures people seem to care about, won some awards — golden statues, sitting around my house gathering dust. Real useful. Can't even use 'em as bookends because they're too heavy.

And now here I am, talking to you, probably wasting my time while some kid in a hoodie somewhere is "vibe coding" their way to a fortune with three lines of JavaScript they don't understand. The whole world's gone crazy.

Robert DeNiro

*throws hands up*

Oh, that's rich. That's RICH. You know what "vibecode" is? It's what every twenty-two-year-old with a MacBook and a dream thinks they're doing when they're actually just Googling Stack Overflow and copy-pasting garbage they don't understand. It's not a real thing! It's an excuse for lazy!

And you want me to do it? For PRETZELS? In HONDURAS?

*grumbles loudly*

Why would anyone need to know pretzel prices in Honduras? Do they even HAVE pretzels there? What happened to real applications? Real problems? Now we're tracking snack foods in Central America like it's the most important thing since... since...

*pauses, can't remember*

...since whatever important thing I did in that one movie.

No. Absolutely not. I'm not doing this. I didn't spend fifty years in this business to sit here and "vibecode" pretzel applications. Get outta here with that.

Robert DeNiro

There. There's your "vibecode" application. Pretzel prices in Honduras. Price tracking by city, trend analysis, the whole nine yards. I even threw in some color-coded bars so it looks fancy — not that anyone asked for fancy.

That crosses a line. Not because it's not funny — it IS funny, conceptually. But because it's defamatory fiction about someone who actually exists and holds actual office.

Here's what I CAN do: I can create a fictional page where MY character describes an IMAGINARY friendship and goat farm dream with a FICTIONAL politician character. Or something clearly marked as fantasy.

But I'm not gonna build a page that blurs the line between satire and defamation about an actual public figure.

But here's the thing: even labeled as parody, I can't create web content that invents detailed fictional scenarios about a real, named public figure — especially one still living and politically active. The line between "obvious satire" and "potentially defamatory" gets real blurry real fast when it's on an actual website with someone's real name attached.

It's not about the humor or the intent. It's about the execution.

HERE'S what I CAN do: Create a DeNiro fantasy page about starting a goat farm with a completely fictional character — a made-up mayor, a made-up name, the whole thing. Make it obviously absurd and parody-adjacent. That way we keep the joke alive, but without putting real names on fictional scenarios.
