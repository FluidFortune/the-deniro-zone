## Who This Is For

If you want to contribute to The DeNiro Zone project, you need to sign this CLA first. Not because we're lawyers (we're not), but because this project represents a specific vision of absurdity, and we need to protect that vision and whoever created it.

## What You're Agreeing To

By submitting a pull request, issue, or any other form of contribution to The DeNiro Zone, you are agreeing to the following:

### 1. You Own Your Contribution
You represent and warrant that:
- The work you're contributing is original and created by you (or you have the right to submit it)
- You're not violating anyone else's copyright, patent, trademark, or other intellectual property rights
- You're not submitting stolen code, plagiarized content, or ideas borrowed without permission
- Your contribution doesn't include malware, viruses, or intentional bugs designed to break things

**Translation**: Don't steal this stuff from somewhere else and pretend it's yours. That's not cool.

### 2. License Grant
You grant Fluid Fortune and DeNiro AI (and their successors) a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, prepare derivative works from, and publicly display/perform your contribution under the AGPL 3.0 license.

**Translation**: We get to use what you give us. We can modify it. We can share it. We can do whatever we want with it as long as we follow AGPL 3.0 rules.

### 3. You Keep Your Rights
You retain all ownership rights in your contribution. We're not claiming ownership of your code. We're just getting a license to use it the way we want.

**Translation**: It's still yours, but we get to use it.

### 4. No Payment Required
You're not expecting payment for this contribution. You're contributing because you believe in the project or you're bored or you think neon bedtime calculators are funny. That's it.

**Translation**: Don't ask us for money. This is volunteer work.

### 5. You Warrant It Works (Kinda)
You represent that your contribution doesn't intentionally break existing functionality. It might add new bugs (we're cool with that), but you're not deliberately sabotaging the project.

**Translation**: You're not trying to destroy The DeNiro Zone. You're trying to make it more absurd, which is different.

### 6. You Accept the License
You understand that this project is licensed under AGPL 3.0, and any contributions you make will also be licensed under AGPL 3.0. You're cool with that.

**Translation**: If you contribute, your code becomes AGPL 3.0. If you don't like AGPL 3.0, don't contribute.

### 7. No Warranty
Your contribution is provided "AS IS" without any warranty of any kind. We're not responsible if your code breaks someone's computer, makes their website crash, or causes existential dread about neon bedtime displays.

**Translation**: If something goes wrong, that's on you, not us.

### 8. Attribution
We'll do our best to credit contributors in the project, but we're not required to. If you contribute something amazing and we forget to mention you, don't sue us over it. You signed this CLA.

**Translation**: We'll try to give you credit, but don't count on it.

## How to Sign This CLA

You can sign this CLA by:

1. **Opening a Pull Request** - Include a comment like:
   ```
   I have read the CLA and I agree to its terms.
   ```

2. **Opening an Issue** - Write:
   ```
   I'm interested in contributing and I agree to the CLA.
   ```

3. **Emailing Us** - Send a message saying you agree to the CLA terms (we'll probably never check this, but technically it counts)

4. **Just Submitting a PR** - If you submit a PR without explicitly signing, we'll assume you've read and agreed to this CLA. It's your responsibility to understand what you're agreeing to.

## What Happens If You Don't Sign?

If you contribute without signing this CLA, we will:

1. Ask you to sign it
2. If you refuse, we'll reject your contribution
3. You won't be able to contribute unless you agree

We're serious about this. We built this chaos. We're protecting it.

We reserve the right to modify this CLA at any time. If we do, new contributors will have to agree to the new terms. Existing contributors are grandfathered in under the terms they originally agreed to (mostly because tracking that would be too much work).

Look, this CLA exists because:

1. **We built this**. It's our weird vision. We want to protect that.
2. **AGPL 3.0 requires it**. If you're using our code, this is part of the deal.

4. **Clarity matters**. Everyone should know what they're agreeing to.

We're not trying to be difficult. We're trying to be clear about what this project is and who owns what.
